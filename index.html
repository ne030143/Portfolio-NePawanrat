Translation from Hand Symbols into Letters

The project utilized the Jetson Nano board for processing and Python Language for running the program. It used a webcam to capture images of hand gestures and compares them with the trained model. The training dataset consisted of a total of 2,600 images, divided into 26 categories (A-Z) with 100 images per category. The program displayed the resulting letters on the screen. Additionally, the project involved studying machine learning theories, utilizing Mediapipe Hand Tracking, and SVM to aid in training the model to achieve accurate and desired results.
